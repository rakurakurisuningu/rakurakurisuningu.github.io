How to use らくらくリスニング

This web-based listening exercise is designed for years 11 & 12 students to practice their listening skills at their own pace. Each task contains
·        an audio file
·        questions
·        vocabulary list and
·        sample answers
To assist students avoid common errors, more than 80 "preparation activities" have been newly added. This section includes not only listening exercises but also lots of games. (shown by ) and a video clip (shown by ). Most of the preparation activities are easy and simple enough for junior to middle year students.
It is very easy to use this programme. Print out the check lists & score records first so that you can find out what activities in this CD-Rom.
 
1. Simply, click on the task number you would like to try. The number of stars shows the difficulty of the task. (* is year 11 level, ** is year 11-12 level and *** is year 12 level or above.)
2. Print out the page if you would like to write your answers down. The answering space is adjusted to respond in hand writing.
3. Read the instruction of the task and questions paying attention to question words.
4. Click CD walkman icon, , to start an audio file. You may listen to the same text twice. Pause 1-2 minutes between first and second listening depending on the length of the text. You may use a dictionary and make notes at any time.
5. To start the second listening, simply click the CD walkman icon again. You may spend 2-3 minutes to respond after the second listening of Part A. For the Part B questions, 10-15 minutes can be used.
6. Scroll down the page to check your answer. The solution is very user friendly.  It contains a vocabulary list, a full listening script and sample answer. The sentences relating to answers are underlined on the script. Students are recommended to learn all the necessary vocabulary referring to the list and try the same text until they feel confident with all the details of the task.   

 

Other usages of  らくらくリスニング

Advanced learners can listen to the text and summarize the whole content.

Advanced learners may also wish to use this programme to improve their dictation skills. (Listen to a text and write what they said in correct Japanese sentences. You would probably need to stop the audio after each sentence.) This will help their skills to spell words correctly.

Some questions might be a little difficult and more suitable to be used as reading tasks.

Content, language, paragraphing  used in らくらくリスニング are all very high quality. Students will find them a good model for their essay practice.
