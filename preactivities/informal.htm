A. Basic Expressions
　
A1. Rules
　
　Casual speech is simply expressed by plain forms and often particles are omitted.
　Female sometimes add わ(よ), の(よ), etc after plain forms, while male sometimes use よ, だよ, etc. However these are not so clearly distinguished these days.
　Both かしら and かなあ/かな means “I wonder/ it is probably”, but かしら can be used only by female.
　To make a question sentence, males often use plain form + (の)か or の in informal situation, but females can use only の.
